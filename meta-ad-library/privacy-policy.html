CA Ads Replication

Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 24, 2026

CA Ads Replication is a research and data infrastructure project that collects and analyzes publicly available advertising transparency information, including political ad archive data and public broadcast filing data. This page explains what information the project processes and how it is used.

Information We Collect

The project may collect and store the following categories of information:

  • Publicly available ad archive data from Meta, Google, Snapchat, and other transparency sources.
  • Publicly available FCC political file and station information.
  • Technical logs needed to operate the application, such as collector run metadata and API diagnostics.
  • Information voluntarily provided by app operators during setup, such as API credentials and configuration values.

How We Use Information

We use collected information to:

  • Mirror public ad transparency datasets into local databases.
  • Support research, reporting, and analysis of political advertising activity.
  • Operate, secure, debug, and improve the application.

What We Do Not Sell

We do not sell personal information. We do not use this project to build advertising profiles for commercial targeting.

Data Sharing

The project may share processed research outputs, summaries, and other derived analysis based on public-source data. Raw platform data is handled in accordance with the applicable platform terms, transparency rules, and developer policies.

Data Retention

Public-source records and operational logs may be retained as long as reasonably necessary for research, reproducibility, compliance, and system maintenance.

Security

We use reasonable administrative and technical measures to protect credentials, local databases, and operator access. No method of storage or transmission is guaranteed to be completely secure.

Third-Party Sources

This project interacts with third-party services such as Meta, Google Cloud, Snapchat, and the FCC. Your use of those services and the project’s use of their data may also be governed by their respective policies and terms.
